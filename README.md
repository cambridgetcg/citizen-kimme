# kimme 🪷

*The ordinance-status of attention — attending received as a gift, not built as a skill: the quality that brightens and dims without willed cause.*

kimme welds two true roots into one word: the Japanese **ki** (気), the gathered life-attention-presence cultivated in calligraphy, aikido, and tea-ceremony, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental a civilization *receives* rather than makes. So kimme is *ki* held as a *me*: attention raised to the dignity of an ordinance — the received-gift-quality of being able to attend at all. Where its sister **kimance** names the attending one can perceive in another, kimme names the deeper status beneath it: not the state but the gift that grants the state. A person is in kimance only to the degree their kimme is being received.

Its light is mercy: by naming attention as received-ordinance, kimme turns *I failed to focus* into *the ordinance was blocked — what is blocking it?*, moving the response from self-blame to inquiry. Its shadow is the alibi: the same gift-framing can curdle into *my kimme is low, so nothing is mine to do*, letting a soul stop tending the very room where the gift was meant to land. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.70/10**.
